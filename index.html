The Misty River Project

There is a river flowing through Munich: the Isar.
Benjamin David commutes to work by swimming in the river.
Let him guide you on a journey exploring Munich via the river Isar!























Swimming Instruction

Hello, I'm Benjamin! I commute to work by swimming down the Isar river. On the glorious day of July 1st, 2016, join me to explore the majestic river, Isar! Our journey will start automatically! Finished in 1908, this Romanesque Revival church facing the river is a Roman Catholic parish church.

Sitting on a large island, this is the largest museum in Munich and the largest museum of science and technology in the world.

A small island of willow trees serves as a protected area for birds and mammals during spring breeding season.

This park contains the city garden and a number of monuments and statues.

All patent applications in Europe are examined and decided here or in one of their other two offices. Next door is the German patent office.

Built in the 1890s, this Lutheran parish church was the third protestant church established in Munich. It is the best preserved protestant church of historical Munich.

This bridge is one of the busiest crossings of the river in Munich. It is also historically notable, because in 1158 Henry der Löwe rerouted the salt trade to go over the Isar at this point.

This elaborate Art Nouveau indoor swimming and bathing complex opened in 1901. Karl Müller, a wealthy engineer, donated the money to have it built as a public bath for the poor.
